NO MORE HUNGER

Top-down, aid-driven charity models fail to create lasting change in the lives of those in need. To achieve a sustainable solution to hunger, we need to flip that model on its head, and afford those living in hunger access to the education and tools needed to end hunger for good.

A Global, Non-Profit, Strategic Organisation

The Hunger Project (THP) is committed to ending hunger and extreme poverty. It runs programmes across 12 countries in Africa, Latin America and South Asia.
In India, the aim is to encourage transformative leadership through a holistic approach.

THP India firmly believes that the active participation of women in decision-making processes related to local governance will deepen democracy and ensure inclusive development, which is socially just and gender equal.
OUR OBJECTIVES
Advocate for policies and programs that address the root causes of hunger and promote food security.
Our strategies and interventions recognise the unique problems that are faced by the most vulnerable and marginalised communities in accessing their rights and entitlements, and we focus on how elected women representatives can exercise their power to address these.

Empower Communities
Empower communities through education, resources, and support to achieve food security and self-sufficiency.

Reduce Food Waste
Minimize food waste at every stage of the supply chain to ensure more efficient use of resources.

Promote Sustainable Agriculture
Support sustainable agricultural practices to increase food production and resilience to climate change.

Eradicate Hunger
Eliminate hunger and malnutrition by ensuring access to sufficient and nutritious food for all.
FACTS AND STUDIES

Nearly one in ten people around the world go to bed hungry each night, a crisis driven largely by conflict, climate change, and chronic inequality. Children Die Each Day from Illnesses Caused by Dirty Water and Unhygienic Living Conditions.


Global Hunger

As of recent estimates, over 820 million people globally are undernourished, with the highest prevalence in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia.

Children Affected

Approximately 149 million children under five are stunted due to chronic undernutrition, impacting their growth and cognitive development.

Poverty Connection

Hunger is closely linked to poverty, with 75% of the world's poorest living in rural areas and dependent on agriculture.


Economic Impact

Malnutrition costs the global economy approximately $3.5 trillion annually due to lost productivity and healthcare costs.



A Far-Reaching Impact.

Across the globe, our education, agriculture, health and microfinance programs impact people with the skills, knowledge and resources they need to break the poverty cycle themselves.
